# VitalNet — Glossary

Domain and project-specific terms used throughout the codebase and docs,
for developers unfamiliar with Indian rural healthcare delivery or with
this project's specific vocabulary.

## Clinical / healthcare domain

**ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist)** — a community health worker in
India's public health system, typically the first point of contact for
rural patients. Not a doctor or nurse — trained in basic health screening,
referral, and community health education. VitalNet's `asha_worker` role
represents this user.

**ASHA Facilitator** — India's National Health Mission role that provides
"supportive supervision" to a cluster of ~10-25 ASHA workers: joint home
visits, monthly cluster meetings, performance support. Reports through a
parallel, non-clinical line to block-level NHM administration — not through
a treating doctor. VitalNet's `supervisor` role is modeled directly on this
real-world role (`docs/DECISIONS.md` §25), which is why it is a distinct
fourth role rather than a permission bolted onto `doctor` or `admin`.

**PHC (Primary Health Centre)** — the first-tier government healthcare
facility in rural India, typically staffed by one or a few doctors, serving
a cluster of villages. A `facilities` row with `type = 'PHC'` represents
one. Larger facilities (CHC — Community Health Centre, district hospitals)
are also represented via `facilities.type`, a free-text field.

**Triage** — the process of sorting patients by the urgency of their
condition so limited clinical attention goes to the most critical cases
first. VitalNet's triage tiers, in descending urgency:
- **EMERGENCY** — immediate, life-threatening; needs review within minutes.
- **URGENT** — needs clinical attention soon, not immediately life-threatening.
- **ROUTINE** — can wait for normal-priority review.

**Chief complaint** — the primary symptom or reason a patient is seeking
care, as reported by the patient/worker (e.g. "chest pain," "high fever").
Distinct from the eventual diagnosis.

**Vitals** — objective physiological measurements: blood pressure (systolic/
diastolic), SpO2 (blood oxygen saturation, %), heart rate (bpm), and body
temperature. Often incomplete in this app's real usage (an ASHA worker may
lack a BP cuff or pulse oximeter) — the ML pipeline is explicitly designed
to degrade gracefully on missing vitals rather than fail.

**SpO2** — peripheral oxygen saturation, measured by a pulse oximeter.
Below ~90% is clinically concerning; below ~85% is an immediate red flag
VitalNet's safety net treats as an automatic EMERGENCY escalation
regardless of what the ML model predicts.

**NEWS2 (National Early Warning Score 2)** — a standardized UK clinical
scoring system that assigns a risk score to each vital sign individually
and sums them to flag patient deterioration. VitalNet borrows the
per-parameter scoring concept (not the full NEWS2 protocol) for its
"never return ROUTINE if any single vital scores concerning" safety floor
— see `docs/DECISIONS.md` §3.

**Differential diagnosis** — a ranked list of plausible conditions that
could explain a patient's presentation, generated by the LLM briefing step
for the reviewing doctor's consideration — not a definitive diagnosis.

**Disposition** (patient disposition) — what actually happened to a patient
after care: `treated_discharged`, `admitted`, `referred_higher_facility`,
`deceased`, or `unknown`. Recorded via the case-outcome endpoint as the
real-world ground truth the retraining loop learns from.

**Consent capture** — the recorded confirmation that a patient (or
guardian) agreed to data collection and AI-assisted triage before a case is
submitted. Enforced server-side (`IntakeForm.consent_captured` must be
`true`), not just a frontend checkbox.

**PHI (Protected Health Information)** — patient data that must be
handled with special care (access-logged, access-controlled, never exposed
in error messages/logs beyond coarse identifiers). See `docs/SECURITY.md`.

## VitalNet-specific technical terms

**Safety net** — the deterministic (non-ML) rule layer that force-escalates
unambiguous critical presentations to EMERGENCY regardless of what the
trained model predicts. See `docs/DECISIONS.md` §3 and
`backend/app/ml/README.md`.

**Golden vectors / golden-vector parity test** — a fixed set of synthetic
patient inputs with their expected computed outputs (either tree-evaluation
results or engineered-feature values), used to mechanically verify the
Python and JS implementations of the same logic produce identical output.
"Parity" here specifically means *online (Python/server) and offline
(JS/browser) triage agree* — the single most safety-critical guarantee in
this codebase. See `docs/TESTING_STRATEGY.md`.

**Feature engineering / `ClinicalFeatureEngineer`** — the process (and the
class that implements it) of expanding ~14 raw intake fields (age, sex,
vitals, symptoms) into 45 derived numeric features the trained model
actually consumes (e.g. `cardiac_risk`, `pediatric_adjustment`,
`time_of_day_risk`). Mirrored 1:1 in JS (`buildFeatureMap()` in
`triageClassifier.js`) — see `docs/DECISIONS.md` §2.

**Tree evaluator** — the ~120-line dependency-free JS function that walks a
compact JSON representation of the trained decision-tree ensemble
(`triage_trees.json`) to reproduce the server model's prediction entirely
client-side, without any ML runtime (no onnxruntime/WASM). See
`docs/DECISIONS.md` §2.

**Low confidence / abstention flag** — a boolean the classifier attaches to
a prediction when the top-class probability is below a tuned threshold or
the top-two classes are close — signals "model uncertain, clinician review
recommended" rather than presenting a low-quality prediction as confident.

**Model card** — `backend/app/ml/MODEL_CARD.md`: the honest, standardized
record of a trained model's intended use, training data provenance,
metrics (and what they do/don't mean), known limitations, and safety-net
guarantees. Concept borrowed from the ML-ethics/responsible-AI literature
(Mitchell et al., "Model Cards for Model Reporting").

**SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)** — a game-theoretic method for
attributing a model's prediction to its input features ("this feature
pushed the prediction toward EMERGENCY by X"). VitalNet uses
`shap.TreeExplainer` to generate the "primary risk driver" explanation
shown to doctors — real attributions of the model's own decision, not a
hand-written heuristic.

**Idempotency key** — a client-generated identifier (`client_id`, a UUID)
attached to a case submission so that retrying the same submission (e.g.
after an offline-queue retry) doesn't create a duplicate record — the
backend upserts on this key. See `docs/DECISIONS.md` §6.

**Offline-first** — a design approach where the app is built to work
without network connectivity as the primary case, with online behavior as
an enhancement, rather than the reverse. ASHA workers may have no
connectivity for extended periods; the intake form, local triage, and
submission queue all function fully offline.

**PWA (Progressive Web App)** — a web app that can be installed on a
device's home screen and work offline via a service worker, without going
through an app store. VitalNet is built as a PWA (`vite-plugin-pwa`) rather
than a native app for this reason (see `FEATURES_ROADMAP.md`'s "explicitly
out of scope" section for the full rationale).

**Realtime** (Supabase Realtime) — a Postgres feature (via logical
replication) that lets clients subscribe to live INSERT/UPDATE events on a
table. Powers the doctor dashboard's live case feed and referral-status
updates without polling.

**Cursor-based / keyset pagination** — a pagination technique using the
last-seen row's sort-key values (rather than an offset/page number) as the
"continue from here" cursor for the next page. Used throughout the API
(`GET /api/cases`, `/api/cases/mine`) — stable under concurrent
inserts/deletes, unlike offset pagination.

**Role scoping** — the four-role authorization model: `admin` (global),
`doctor` (scoped to their own facility), `supervisor` (scoped to their own
facility, aggregate-only, non-PHI — see ASHA Facilitator below and
`docs/DECISIONS.md` §25), `asha_worker` (scoped to their own submissions).
See `docs/API_REFERENCE.md`'s conventions section and CODEBASE_MAP.md §6.

**Hybrid auth** — VitalNet's JWT verification approach: local signature
verification on the hot path, with a network fallback only when local
verification can't apply. See `docs/DECISIONS.md` §1.

**Retraining loop / outcome feedback loop** — the mechanism by which real
doctor corrections (triage overrides) and recorded patient outcomes become
labeled training data for a future model retrain
(`scripts/retrain_from_outcomes.py`), blended with synthetic data, never
auto-deployed (human-gated promotion).

**Tier 1 / 1b / 2 / 3** — `FEATURES_ROADMAP.md`'s prioritization framework.
Tier 1: fixes a real patient-safety/data-integrity/operational-trust gap,
feasible without a major new subsystem. Tier 2: clear value, larger effort
or a new dependency. Tier 3: high value but needs a product/vendor decision
before implementation specs are even meaningful (e.g. SMS-fallback,
photo attachments).

## General technical terms (for context, not VitalNet-specific)

**RLS (Row Level Security)** — a PostgreSQL feature restricting which rows
a query can see/modify based on the connecting role/session, enforced by
the database itself regardless of what the application code does. VitalNet
layers this underneath application-level authorization, not instead of it.

**ADR (Architecture Decision Record)** — a short document capturing a
significant technical decision, its context, and its consequences.
`docs/DECISIONS.md` is VitalNet's ADR log (informally structured, not the
strict Nygard template, but the same intent).

**ERD (Entity-Relationship Diagram)** — a diagram showing database tables
and their relationships. CODEBASE_MAP.md §5 includes one (as a Mermaid
`erDiagram`, rendered natively by GitHub).
